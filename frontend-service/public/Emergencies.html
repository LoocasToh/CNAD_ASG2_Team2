Safety Tips & Important Information

Important things to remember to stay safe

When to call for help

  • If you feel sick or have pain
  • If you fall down and can't get up
  • If you see smoke or fire
  • If someone is trying to hurt you
  • If you feel very scared or confused

Your Helper (Caregiver)

  • Helps with meals and medicine
  • Assists with getting dressed
  • Takes you for walks or outings
  • Helps with bathroom needs
  • Is your main contact for daily help

How to use the call buttons

  • Press once to start a call
  • Timer gives you 30 seconds to talk
  • If timer reaches 0, help is sent
  • Press "Cancel Call" if you're okay
  • Speak clearly when someone answers

Emergency 911

USE ONLY FOR SERIOUS EMERGENCIES

Call 911 ONLY if:

  • There is a fire or smoke
  • Someone is seriously hurt or unconscious
  • You are having trouble breathing
  • Someone is having a seizure
  • There is a crime in progress
  • You see a serious car accident

For non-emergency medical issues, call your helper first.

How to use:

  1. Press the RED button below
  2. You have 30 seconds before automatic call
  3. If you can talk, tell them what's wrong
  4. If you can't talk, stay on the line - help will come
  5. Try to say your name and address if you can

After pressing, if you can't speak within 30 seconds, emergency services will be notified automatically.
